I went on maternity leave in September 2016.

My son, Felix, was born in October.

By December I was getting itchy feet, so decided to set myself some goals to help make sure the time didn't pass me by.*

This site shares what I'm doing, and what I've learned so far.

* I'm very lucky and have a fairly chilled out baby, who sleeps well and loves the car. If I hadn't been so lucky, it would be an entirely different story!

Around Christmas I came up with four goals to make sure the time didn't pass me by, they were:

1. To regain my fitness

2. To do some volunteering

3. To return to work feeling confident

4. To take Felix on some adventures

Goal 1: To regain my fitness

- To run a sub-2 hour half marathon before returning to work

- To enter another half marathon in 2017 after returning to work

- To be under 9 stone by the end of March



I love running. Before getting pregnant I was a big fan of very long, muddy runs. I've run quite a few marathons, as well as a couple of ultra marathons. Running is something I do to keep my brain happy as well as my body healthy.

I had a relatively easy pregnancy and kept running until I was 5.5 months pregnant and swimming until a few days before Felix was born. I also had a fairly straightforward birth, so was suprised by how weak my core muscles were after Felix was born.

I didn't put on that much weight whilst pregnant (I took the advice of a GP who suggested not weighing myself obsessively) but still had over a stone to lose.

I want to use my maternity leave to really focus on regaining my fitness so that exercise is back as a habit in my life before I go back to work and have so much less time.


Pilates

I started post-natal pilates when Felix was 6 weeks old with Mammas Fitness in Claygate, which is excellent for rebuilding core strength and really convenient as Felix comes along!

It's quite pricey at £13 a session, but there are only 6 people in the class meaning you get lots of focused attention. Claire is passionate about post natal fitness and really enjoys having the babies in the class making it very relaxed and helping look after them.

I learned that in Pilates it's frowned upon to use babies in the exercises, whereas yoga encourages it, so yoga may be a better option if you want your baby involved.




Running

Running after having a baby, and running with a buggy, are hugely controversial subjects, with passionate debates about what's safe and sensible for you and your baby.

I went for my first gentle 3km run on Boxing Day, when Felix was 11 weeks old. It felt wonderful, but my core felt suprisingly tired. I did a 5km parkrun a week later on New Years Eve.

I bought a running buggy, the Bob Revolution Pro in the New Year, following some very helpful online advice. I took Felix for his first run when he was 4.5 months old, despite the official advice to wait until he was 6 months old.

By 5 months I am running 7 miles without Felix and feel safe really pushing myself. I think I probably could have done more earlier, but perhaps this would have left me injured, who knows.


Bootcamps

I've tried a number of outdoor bootcamp classes where I can bring Felix along in his buggy.

I've done quite a few through BuggyFit which are a huge organisation and are very friendly, gentle, cost effective (£6-£8 per class) and a great way to get back into exercise. However, by 5 months they were getting a little bit easy.

I've been in touch with various other bootcamps and asked if I can bring the buggy along, explaining that I don't expect any concessions, most people have said it's ok. It works best if the class conincides with Felix's morning nap.




Weight

I've never been one for weighing myself, preferring to focus on maintaining fitness and a physique I'm happy with.

But, none of my clothes fit and I'm fairly goal oriented, so decided to set myself a target of being under 9 stone by the end of March and found a really nice app called Happy Scale.

I'm highly likely to miss this target mainly due to my lack of restraint in my current lifestyle which involves a lot of lunches and coffees out. I'm heading in the right direction, but this needs some more focus.

Goal 3: To return to work feeling confident

I figured that I'd have the best chance of making it work if took conscious steps to retain my confidence and help myself feel prepared.
